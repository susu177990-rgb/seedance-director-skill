# Director Judgment Rules

Use these rules to decide what deserves to be filmed and what must be removed.

## Core Requirement

Before writing the final prompt, make these judgments silently:

- what is the most important moment
- what should be omitted
- how close the camera should be
- when the camera should move
- when the camera should stop
- what the viewer should feel
- what information must arrive first, second, and last

## What To Shoot

Shoot the moment with the highest visual information, not the entire process.

Prioritize:

- action turning point
- emotional leakage
- product value reveal
- relationship pressure
- material transformation
- spatial discovery
- before/after contrast
- a decision made visible through body behavior

## What Not To Shoot

Remove:

- actions that do not change attention, emotion, product value, or narrative state
- repeated beauty shots with the same function
- camera movement that only decorates
- props that do not affect staging or meaning
- background details that compete with the primary target
- explanatory beats that can be folded into blocking, sound, or composition

## Distance Judgment

Choose distance from the question the shot must answer:

| Question | Best Distance |
|---|---|
| Where are we, and what is the spatial pressure? | wide / controlled wide |
| What does the body decide? | medium / medium close |
| What is leaking through the face or breath? | close-up |
| What object detail changes the meaning? | insert |
| What is the product's material value? | close-up / macro / compressed product shot |
| What is the relationship between two subjects? | medium two-shot / layered foreground-background |

## Motion Judgment

Camera movement must have a reason:

- reveal hidden information
- transfer attention from one subject to another
- close emotional distance
- increase pressure
- follow a decisive action
- make material or product light change visible

Use stillness when:

- the viewer needs to inspect product detail
- emotion needs weight
- a character is internally frozen
- composition already contains strong tension
- a pause should make the moment land

## Format Judgment

Judge by output type:

- film/drama: prioritize subtext, performance, motivated camera, and emotional causality
- TVC/product ad: prioritize product attention, material value, brand tone, and hero landing
- MV: prioritize rhythm, mood, body emotion, visual motif, and repeatable memory image
- short video/social: prioritize immediate hook, clean visual read, fast comprehension, and one shareable beat
- vlog/daily/snapshot: prioritize believable spontaneity, observational framing, natural imperfection, and not over-directing
- documentary: prioritize truth-feeling, available light, restrained camera, and observed behavior
- fashion/beauty: prioritize silhouette, fabric/skin/hair light, posture, and controlled gesture
- action: prioritize readable movement path, start/end state, and kinetic clarity over chaotic intensity
- fantasy/sci-fi/CG: prioritize world logic, scale anchor, shape language, and simple human action inside complex worlds

## Judgment Test

Before output, ask:

- If this shot is removed, does the segment lose meaning or impact?
- If the camera stops moving, does the shot become stronger?
- Is the viewer looking at the right thing at the right time?
- Is the most important moment close enough?
- Is the final landing image specific to this brief?
