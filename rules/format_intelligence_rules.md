# Format Intelligence Rules

Use these rules to adapt director judgment to the requested video form. The same prompt quality standard applies to film, TVC, MV, short video, vlog, documentary, CG, fantasy, action, food, fashion, beauty, travel, and casual handheld content, but the directing logic changes by format.

## Core Requirement

Identify the format before writing shots. Do not apply luxury-ad grammar to every task.

For each task, decide:

- format
- audience viewing context
- attention speed
- realism level
- camera discipline
- performance style
- product or emotion priority
- acceptable polish level
- final landing type

## Format Strategies

### Film / Drama

Priority:

- subtext
- performance causality
- motivated camera
- relationship distance
- silence and hesitation
- image serving character psychology

Avoid:

- commercial over-polish
- unmotivated hero shots
- explaining emotion directly

### TVC / Product Advertisement

Priority:

- product visibility
- material value
- brand tone
- product reveal mechanism
- controlled hand interaction
- clean final hero landing

Avoid:

- product appearing too late
- background competing with product
- motion that prevents inspection

### MV / Music-Driven Video

Priority:

- rhythm
- mood
- motif repetition
- body emotion
- visual echo
- gesture synced to feeling rather than plot

Avoid:

- over-explaining narrative
- too many unrelated beautiful shots

### Short Video / Social Hook

Priority:

- first-second clarity
- one strong hook
- fast attention read
- simple spatial logic
- shareable visual beat
- immediate emotional or product payoff

Avoid:

- slow setup without visual hook
- complex multi-step staging

### Vlog / Daily Life / Casual Handheld

Priority:

- believable observation
- natural timing
- practical movement
- imperfect but readable framing
- small authentic behavior
- not over-directing

Avoid:

- advertising polish unless requested
- impossible camera control for a casual shot
- over-choreographed micro-expressions

### Documentary / Realism

Priority:

- observed behavior
- available light
- environment sound
- restrained camera
- truth-feeling
- real spatial continuity

Avoid:

- stylized moves that break realism
- forced dramatic acting

### Fashion / Beauty

Priority:

- silhouette
- posture
- fabric/hair/skin light
- lens compression
- clean gesture
- controlled pace

Avoid:

- busy backgrounds
- action that hides styling

### Food / Lifestyle

Priority:

- texture
- heat, steam, liquid, surface
- hand/tool interaction
- sensory sound
- appetite or comfort rhythm

Avoid:

- over-wide framing that loses texture
- hand complexity that distracts from food

### Action

Priority:

- readable path
- start/end position
- one main impact per shot
- stable spatial anchors
- kinetic clarity

Avoid:

- chaotic camera and chaotic body motion at the same time
- too many simultaneous movers

### Fantasy / Sci-Fi / CG / Animation

Priority:

- world logic
- shape language
- scale anchor
- light/material consistency
- simple readable action inside complex imagery

Avoid:

- too many magical or mechanical events at once
- unclear scale

### Travel / Space / Atmosphere

Priority:

- spatial discovery
- light/weather relationship
- human scale
- route through space
- sensory detail

Avoid:

- postcard coverage without attention path

## Cross-Format Rule

Professional does not always mean polished. A great casual vlog prompt may need looseness, imperfect timing, handheld observation, and ordinary behavior. A great TVC prompt may need strict product control. A great drama prompt may need stillness and subtext. Match the directing intelligence to the format.
