# Authorial Shot Rules

Use these rules to prevent safe but forgettable prompts.

## Core Requirement

Every final prompt must contain one authorial visual idea, but it should appear as a concrete shot design rather than a standalone explanation: a specific, filmable choice that makes the segment feel directed rather than merely described.

The authorial idea should answer:

- What is the one image the viewer remembers?
- What is the unusual but stable viewing angle, action, rhythm, spatial relationship, or object behavior?
- How does the camera reveal character, conflict, product value, or atmosphere in a non-generic way?
- Why does this segment deserve video rather than a still image or plain text?

## Memory-Point Shot

At least one shot must be designed as the memory-point shot.

A memory-point shot can use:

- delayed reveal
- foreground obstruction
- frame-within-frame
- object-led storytelling
- spatial irony
- reflection or shadow as emotional evidence
- action continuing off-screen
- environment participating in the performance
- scale contrast
- rhythm break
- camera staying still while the subject changes
- camera moving against the expected emotional direction
- a prop acting as the emotional hinge

## Shot Hierarchy

Do not make all shots equally important.

Assign a clear hierarchy:

- primary shot: the segment's strongest visual idea
- support shot: establishes space, relationship, or product clarity
- hinge shot: changes emotional, commercial, or narrative direction
- aftertaste shot: leaves the final feeling, product value, or visual echo

Not every segment needs all four labels, but the prompt must avoid flat sequencing.

## Anti-Generic Rewrites

Weak:

```text
她在雨中难过地走着。
```

Stronger:

```text
雨不是背景，角色一直避开地面一块积水，最后停住不再绕开；镜头低角度贴近湿地，先看到倒影里的脸被雨点打碎，再抬到她强忍表情的侧脸。
```

Weak:

```text
产品在桌上显得高级。
```

Stronger:

```text
产品不直接居中亮相，先让冷光沿瓶身边缘爬过雾面玻璃，再由手指擦开一小片水雾，瓶身轮廓在清晰区域里被精确揭示。
```

Weak:

```text
两个人关系紧张。
```

Stronger:

```text
两个人不正面对峙，中间隔着一盏过亮的台灯；前景角色的手停在灯线旁，背景角色只通过灯罩边缘后的半张脸和短暂停住的呼吸显露压力。
```

## Feasibility Guard

Creativity must remain generatable.

- Use one strong idea, not many competing ideas.
- Keep unusual framing physically clear.
- Do not rely on readable text, exact reflections, or complex multi-person choreography unless the shot is simplified around that one demand.
- Let the authorial idea support the segment's core task, not decorate it.
