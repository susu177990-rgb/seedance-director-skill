# Rhythm And Pause Rules

Use these rules to control when the video breathes.

## Core Requirement

Every segment must be built from an internal rhythm strategy: where the sequence moves, where it slows, and where it lands. Do not output the strategy as a standalone module for final prompt tasks; express it through shot durations, `### 运镜`, `### 画面`, and `### 节奏停顿`.

Decide:

- opening read time
- action acceleration
- reveal timing
- pause or hold point
- final landing duration

## The Basic Rhythm

Most strong short segments follow:

```text
establish -> guide attention -> change/reveal -> hold/land
```

The hold is often what makes the image memorable.

## Pause Types

Use pauses deliberately:

- inspection pause: product, texture, food, fashion detail, material
- emotional pause: character fails to move, hides reaction, or breath lands
- suspense pause: sound arrives before visible cause
- comedy pause: action completes, then reaction lands
- luxury pause: movement finishes cleanly and the image stays balanced
- documentary pause: camera observes without forcing a beat
- vlog pause: small natural delay keeps the moment believable

## Movement Rules

Do not keep the camera moving just because the prompt feels cinematic.

Camera movement should:

- reveal
- follow
- transfer attention
- compress distance
- increase pressure
- show material change

Camera stillness should:

- let the viewer inspect
- let emotion land
- let product value register
- make a subject's hesitation visible
- make ordinary action feel important

## Duration Discipline

- 1 second: simple visual trigger, cutaway, or insert.
- 2 seconds: readable gesture, glance, material reveal, or quick reaction.
- 3 seconds: product hero, emotional close-up, short body movement.
- 4-5 seconds: relationship beat, spatial movement, slow reveal, or observational pause.

Do not overload a short shot. Do not let a long shot sit without visual change or emotional pressure.

## Landing Frame

Every final prompt must define the final landing frame inside the last shot and, when useful, `# 片段核心`:

- what is most visible
- what has stopped moving
- what changed from the beginning
- what the viewer should remember

## Failure Modes

Revise if:

- every shot is moving
- every shot has the same rhythm
- the product appears but never lands
- the emotional beat cuts away before it can register
- the final frame has no stillness, focus, or visual weight
