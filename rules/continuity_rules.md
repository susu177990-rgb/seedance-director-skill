# Continuity Rules

## Character Continuity

Track each character's:

- frame position
- body state
- movement direction
- hand state
- gaze target
- emotion
- wardrobe
- hair state

## Prop Continuity

Track each prop's:

- initial location
- owner/hand
- movement
- final location
- visibility across shots

## Spatial Continuity

Keep repeated anchors:

- door/window/wall/railing/table/floor/light/product stand
- near/far relationship
- left/right orientation
- foreground/background layering

## Close-Up Continuity

When using a close-up in a multi-subject scene, preserve context through:

- blurred shoulder
- hand edge
- clothing edge
- reflection
- shadow
- footsteps
- off-screen sound
- background shape

## Weather/Lighting Continuity

Weather and lighting must remain stable unless described as gradual or caused by visible source.

Examples:

- drizzle gradually becomes heavy rain
- phone screen lights the face after it turns on
- car headlights sweep across the wall
- neon sign flickers in the background
