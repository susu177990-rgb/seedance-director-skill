# Mise-en-scene And Staging Rules

Use these rules to make people, products, camera, light, space, props, and action relate to each other.

## Core Requirement

Do not merely place elements in the frame. Make them affect one another.

For each segment, define:

- subject-to-space relationship
- subject-to-prop or product relationship
- camera-to-subject relationship
- light-to-subject or light-to-product relationship
- foreground, midground, and background roles
- movement path and blocking pressure
- what changes in the relationship by the end

## Staging Relationships

Use relationship instead of isolated description:

- product is revealed by hand, reflection, condensation, shadow, packaging, or light
- character is framed by doorway, table edge, window, mirror, crowd, empty space, or another body
- light arrives before the character looks up
- camera waits while the subject enters the composition
- foreground object blocks the easy view and creates depth
- background movement pressures a still subject
- prop creates distance, intimacy, refusal, temptation, or status

## Composition Order

Every strong composition should state:

- subject position
- foreground layer
- midground action
- background anchor
- line or shape direction
- empty space purpose
- what the frame excludes

## Product Staging

For TVC, product ads, beauty, food, fashion, or object-led shots:

- product must have a clear visual weight
- product scale should be intentional
- product light must describe material value
- hand interaction must be simple and elegant
- product reveal should have a mechanism, not just a centered display
- final product frame should have a stable landing point

## Character Staging

For drama, MV, vlog, documentary, short video, or character-led shots:

- distance between characters must mean something
- body orientation must show relationship
- eye lines must guide attention
- posture must reveal status, emotion, or resistance
- entrances and exits must be spatially clear
- off-screen presence can be held through sound, shadow, reflection, or partial body edge

## Naturalistic Staging

For vlog, daily life, documentary, and casual scenes:

- avoid over-polished blocking
- use believable pauses, imperfect timing, and practical movement
- let the camera discover rather than announce
- keep one authentic accident-like detail if it supports realism
- do not turn casual formats into luxury commercials unless requested

## Failure Modes

Revise if:

- objects feel pasted into the frame
- product has no relation to hand, light, material, or space
- characters move without spatial motivation
- foreground/background are decorative only
- the frame has many items but no visual order
