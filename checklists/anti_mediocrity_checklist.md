# Anti-Mediocrity Checklist

Run this silently before final output.

## Authorial Quality

- The segment has one authorial visual idea.
- At least one shot is a memory-point shot.
- The strongest shot is not a generic centered beauty shot unless symmetry is the idea.
- The camera choice reveals story, character, product value, or emotional pressure.
- Environment, prop, light, or framing participates in the meaning.

## Attention Control

- The viewer knows where to look first.
- The second attention target is guided, not accidental.
- The strongest reveal is timed rather than shown immediately by default.
- The final landing frame has a clear visual weight.
- Beautiful details do not compete with the main target.

## Director Judgment

- The shot list removes unnecessary actions, props, and decorative camera moves.
- The most important moment is close enough.
- The format grammar matches the brief rather than defaulting to generic polished cinema.
- Camera movement changes attention, pressure, distance, or information.
- There is at least one deliberate stop, hold, or pause when the moment needs weight.

## Mise-en-scene

- People, products, props, camera, light, and space affect one another.
- Foreground, midground, and background create visual order.
- Product or object value is shown through interaction, light, material, reveal, or scale.
- Character position, distance, and body direction express relationship or pressure.

## Performance Quality

- Abstract emotions are translated into visible behavior.
- Important character beats include gaze, hands, body, and rhythm where visible.
- Facial expression is not described only with broad adjectives.
- Personality appears through repeated action choices, not biography.
- The prompt distinguishes what the character shows from what the character hides.

## Shot Rhythm

- Shots are not equally weighted.
- There is a primary shot, hinge shot, or aftertaste shot.
- Each shot has a function beyond covering the brief.
- The sequence contains a reveal, contrast, pressure change, or emotional turn when appropriate.
- The sequence breathes; it does not move constantly without a landing.

## Rewrite Triggers

Revise before output if any of these appear:

- "cinematic" is the main creative idea.
- The most memorable image could apply to any similar prompt.
- A character is only described as sad, nervous, happy, elegant, angry, or mysterious.
- Product shots only say premium, beautiful, luxury, high-end, clean, or glossy without a visual mechanism.
- Every shot uses normal eye-level framing and direct subject presentation.
- The prompt is complete but has no surprising, precise, filmable choice.
- The viewer attention path cannot be described.
- The camera keeps moving without a reason.
- The scene contains many elements but no staging relationship.
- A vlog, documentary, or casual brief is over-polished into a commercial.
