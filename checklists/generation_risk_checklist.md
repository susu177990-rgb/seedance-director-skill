# Generation Risk Checklist

Use this to diagnose or rewrite unstable Seedance prompts.

## Common Risk Signals

- Too many actions in one shot.
- Too many people moving at once.
- Character changes location without transition.
- Prop appears/disappears.
- Tiny hand detail is critical to the plot.
- Face is hidden while identity consistency matters.
- Camera motion and actor motion fight each other.
- Scene contains mirror/glass/reflection logic without anchors.
- Product/character is not visible enough.
- Shot is emotionally abstract but visually vague.

## Rewrite Fixes

- Split complex motion into two shots.
- Keep one main action per shot.
- Add strong spatial anchors.
- Add clear start/end body position.
- Use inserts for important props.
- Replace abstract emotion with visible behavior.
- Reduce background complexity.
- Keep camera movement slower and more motivated.
- Use off-screen sound or blurred edges for continuity.
